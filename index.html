The Last Harness You'll Ever Build

Seong, Yin & Zhang (Sylph.AI) · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.21003

Can a two-level optimization loop — an adversarial Harness Evolution Loop driving task-level harness improvement, and a Meta-Evolution Loop learning a reusable blueprint across tasks — eliminate human harness engineering entirely? The adversarial three-agent architecture (Worker, adversarial Evaluator, and Evolution Agent with full-history tracking) is a verified novel design, but both algorithms are currently unimplementable: the Evaluator's mechanism is unspecified, the modification search space has no validity conditions, and the outer loop contains no stopping criterion — gaps that require either an empirical demonstration or formal convergence analysis to resolve.

ESRRSim

Kumarage, Bauer, Ma et al. (Amazon Nova) · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.22119

ESRRSim introduces a verified-novel four-phase automated pipeline for behavioral risk evaluation of LLMs, including a Thought Response Rubric that scores chain-of-thought for strategic risk indicators — genuine methodological advances in a high-stakes domain. The central open challenge: the covert elicitation design premise is contested by a paper the authors themselves cite, and no controlled experiment yet distinguishes genuinely safer models from those that are better at recognizing evaluation contexts.

Temporally Extended MoEs

Shen & Henderson (Princeton) · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.20156

Can the options framework from reinforcement learning tame the near-maximal expert switch rates that cripple MoE offloading? The paper credibly reduces switch rates from >50% to <5% via a lightweight RL-trained controller — a verified novel contribution — but cannot yet establish whether the options framework itself or standard fine-tuning drives the accuracy preservation, since the discriminating ablation is explicitly deferred to future work.

ShadowPEFT

X. Li, Z. Li, A. Lee, J. Li et al. · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.19254

ShadowPEFT introduces the first sequential cross-layer GRU shadow state in PEFT, achieving meaningful gains on reasoning (GSM8K +2.35 pp) and OOD transfer (SQuAD V2 +2.88 pp). The central open question is whether these advantages hold under fairly optimized baselines — code analysis reveals learning-rate asymmetry of 5–15× on the two strongest benchmarks, and the five-run averaging stated in Table 1 is absent from the released training scripts.

SNR-t Bias in Diffusion Models

Yu, Sun, Zeng, Chu, Zhan · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.16044

Does inference-time SNR-timestep misalignment silently degrade diffusion model quality? This CVPR 2026 paper isolates SNR-t bias as a distinct phenomenon from exposure bias and proposes a wavelet-domain correction achieving up to 47% FID improvement across 8 architectures with only 0.08–0.47% overhead, but the core theoretical assumption lacks rigorous validation in low-step regimes, gains shrink to marginal on already-optimized models, and per-model hyperparameter tuning undermines the plug-and-play positioning.

STOP: Learning to Prune Paths Early for Efficient Parallel Reasoning

Bi, Luo, Du, Tang, Wang · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.16029

Can a lightweight learned token probe internal hidden states to prune futile reasoning paths before they waste compute? STOP provides the first taxonomy of path pruning and a 0.59%-overhead pruner achieving 70%+ token savings across 1.5B–20B models, but its own ablation shows only a 0.42% accuracy edge over retrained external methods, no variance is reported on 30-problem evaluation sets, and gains diminish sharply with model scale.

Reinforcement Learning via Value Gradient Flow

Xu, Hu, Sojoudi, Zhang · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.14265

Can SVGD in action space without policy parameterization solve behavior-regularized RL as optimal transport? VGF introduces a verified-novel particle-based mechanism with exceptional hard-task OGBench results (cube-double +50 pts, puzzle-3x3 d=15.4), but carries six CRITICAL findings: N=10 used while N=5 claimed, a core hyperparameter undisclosed across its 20x range, and an entirely unreproducible RLHF contribution.

HiVLA: A Visual-Grounded-Centric Hierarchical Embodied Manipulation System

Yang, Chen, Chen, Liang et al. · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.14125

Can hierarchical decoupling of a frozen VLM planner and a flow-matching DiT action expert preserve VLM reasoning while enabling fine-grained robotic manipulation? HiVLA introduces two verified-novel mechanisms — absolute sinusoidal PE on local crop tokens (-65 pp ablation collapse) and cascaded G→L→T cross-attention — embedded in a paper whose central catastrophic-forgetting claim is tautological by construction, with 6 of 15 training tasks unreported and no variance anywhere in the experiments.

Neural Computers

Zhuge et al. (Meta AI, KAUST) · April 2026 · arXiv:2604.06425

Can a single neural network replace a physical computer's runtime? This paper introduces the Neural Computer (NC) abstraction and two prototypes: NC_CLIGen for terminal simulation and NC_GUIWorld for desktop GUI. We found a verified novel domain contribution in CLI simulation and a decisive training insight (SVG cursor supervision: 8.7% → 98.7%), alongside significant gaps in closed-loop evaluation and citation coverage.
